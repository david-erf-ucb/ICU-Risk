The Problem

ICUs are flying blind.

Dr. Robby is making life-or-death calls based on vitals that are already old — filled in manually, by a nurse already running between six beds.

📋

Manual spreadsheets

By the time a vital sign is typed in, it is already out of date. If the unit is short-staffed, it may not be updated at all.

⏱

No early warning

Existing scoring systems like SOFA and qSOFA look at a single snapshot. They cannot see whether a patient is deteriorating or stabilising.

👥

Too many patients

An attending physician cannot hold the full trajectory of 20 patients in their head at once. Who needs attention next is unclear.

📊

Missed deterioration

Sepsis affects 37% of ICU patients. Mortality affects 15%. Late identification costs lives and drives average costs to over $51,000 per stay.

How It Works

Every patient. Every risk.
No spreadsheet required.

Vital Connect reads live ICU data and produces a ranked, colour-coded risk dashboard that updates automatically.

01

Ingest live data

Vitals, labs, and interventions stream in hourly. Frequency of measurement and missingness are encoded as features — signals most systems ignore.

02

Score with LSTM

A two-layer LSTM with temporal attention reads 24 hours of patient history. It learns which hours matter most — not just what the values were.

03

Surface the risk

Every patient is ranked by combined sepsis and mortality risk. On the floor: scan the table. At the desk: click a patient for their full 24-hour trajectory.

The Model

Two models. One pipeline.
Smaller, stronger.

01 — KEY FINDING

Pipeline over architecture

Z-score normalisation produced the single largest AUPRC jump — bigger than any model change.

02 — KEY FINDING

Temporal beats flat

An LSTM with attention learns which hours matter. A flat vector loses that entirely.

03 — KEY FINDING

Smaller, stronger

350K parameters outperforms the 5.7M baseline on AUPRC. Scale is not the answer.

04 — KEY FINDING

AUROC misleads

A model predicting survival always gets 85% accuracy. AUPRC tells the clinical truth.

Roadmap

From prototype
to clinical tool.

Three steps between where we are and a system a hospital can actually trust.

01

EHR integration via FHIR

Connect to Epic, Cerner, or Meditech via HL7 FHIR R4. Turns the demo into a live clinical product with real patients and real data.

Next step
02

Clinician feedback loop

Every alert override is a training signal. Quarterly retraining on live data closes the gap between MIMIC and the deployed hospital population.

In design
03

Prospective shadow validation

Run the model live without showing clinicians the output. Measure whether earlier predictions would have changed care decisions.
